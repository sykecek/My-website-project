DEBRIS-DISK SYSTEMS

Hubble Space Telescope observation of the debris ring around Fomalhaut. The inner edge of the disk may have been shaped by the orbit of Fomalhaut b, at lower right

A debris-disk system is a star system surrounded by a belt of dust and small bodies produced by collisions—similar to our asteroid belt and Kuiper Belt, but sometimes much brighter and easier to detect.

Debris disks are important because they trace ongoing collisional activity and reveal where planetesimals (and often planets) reside.

What we learn from them

  • Where the belts are: rings, gaps, and clumps can hint at unseen planets.
  • System age: dust often declines over time as collisions grind bodies down.
  • Architecture: multiple belts suggest multiple formation zones.
