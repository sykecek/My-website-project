COMPACT MULTI-PLANET SYSTEMS

Compact multi-planet systems contain several planets orbiting very close to their star—often within the distance of Mercury’s orbit in our Solar System. Many have tightly packed, nearly coplanar orbits.

These systems are common in exoplanet surveys and are especially frequent around small, cool stars. Their spacing and resonances provide strong constraints on how planets migrate and form in disks.

What makes them special

  • Tight spacing: planets can be separated by only a few percent in orbital distance.
  • Resonant chains: many show period ratios close to simple fractions.
  • Transit-friendly: compact, flat orbits make transits more likely to be seen.
