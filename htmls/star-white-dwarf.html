WHITE DWARFS

A white dwarf is the dense leftover core of a Sun-like star after it has shed its outer layers. White dwarfs are roughly Earth-sized but can contain a mass comparable to the Sun.

They no longer fuse elements in their cores. Instead, they slowly cool over time, fading gradually for billions of years.

Planetary nebula phase

Before a white dwarf is left behind, the dying star can create a glowing shell of gas called a planetary nebula. (Despite the name, it’s not related to planets.)
