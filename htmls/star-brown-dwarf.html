BROWN DWARFS

Brown dwarfs are objects between planets and stars. They form like stars (from collapsing gas) but they don’t have enough mass to sustain long-term hydrogen fusion in their cores.

Some brown dwarfs can briefly fuse deuterium or lithium depending on their mass, but they gradually cool and fade over time.

Why they’re hard to see

Because brown dwarfs are cool and dim, they’re often found using infrared surveys rather than visible light.
