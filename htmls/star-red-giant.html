RED GIANTS

A red giant is a late evolutionary stage for many stars. After the star exhausts hydrogen in its core, the core contracts and heats up while the outer layers expand dramatically.

Red giants become much larger and brighter than they were on the main sequence, even though their surface temperatures are cooler (which gives them a reddish color).

The Sun’s future

In several billion years, the Sun is expected to expand into a red giant. Later it will shed its outer layers and leave behind a white dwarf.
