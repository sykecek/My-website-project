MAIN SEQUENCE STARS

The main sequence is the long, stable phase where a star fuses hydrogen into helium in its core. Most stars you see in the night sky are main sequence stars.

A star’s position on the main sequence depends mostly on its mass. Higher-mass stars are hotter and brighter but live much shorter lives; lower-mass stars are cooler and dimmer but can last for trillions of years.

The Sun

The Sun is a G-type main sequence star. It has been steadily fusing hydrogen for about 4.6 billion years and is roughly halfway through its main sequence lifetime.
