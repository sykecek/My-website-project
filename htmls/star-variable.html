VARIABLE STARS

Variable stars change brightness over time. Some pulse because their outer layers expand and contract. Others vary because they are in binary systems that eclipse each other.

Variable stars are important in astronomy: certain types (like Cepheids) are used to measure distances across the universe.

Why they help measure the universe

For some pulsating stars, the period of brightness changes is linked to their true luminosity. By comparing true luminosity to observed brightness, astronomers can estimate distance.
